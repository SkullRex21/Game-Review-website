The description of the game

The game is made by Quantic Dream and directed by David Cage. Quantic Dream also made Detroit Become Human and Beyond Two Souls, David Cage directed both of them as well.

The game is about the Origami Killer who kiddnaps and kills kids and uses rain water to drown them.
The victims are found dead four days after they go missing, the only clues are an origami figure and an orchid placed on their or near their bodies.
The four main characters are Scott Shelby, Norman Jayden, Ethan Mars and Madison Paige.

They are each tied to the Origami Killer, Ethan is a father and his son Shaun got kiddnapped by the Origami Killer, he is trying to find his son with the clues the Killer has left for him.
Madison is the least connected to the Killer she is a journalist and is write about the killer and the victim families, she comes across Ethan and starts talking to him trying to get information.
Norman is a FBI agent and as been assigned to the Origami Killer case at the local police station, he is trying to catch the Killer and bring justice to the victim families.
Scott is a private detective who is also trying to find the Origami Killer and he is working with some of the victim families to help them.

The development of the game

The game was first pitched to Microsoft, but it was rejected for its themes of child abduction so they turned to Sony who help them with development. At E3 2006 Quantic Dream revealed a tech demo of the game running on the PS3, the demo was made in three months including the game engine, script, casting and motion capture. The actual game took four years to make and had a budget of Â£35 million, on set there were seventy actors and stuntmen and 30,000 animations. Heavy Rain had been scheduled for a 2008 release but it got delayed to the 23rd of February 2010, leading up to the release there were marketing campaign that had people finding four pieces of evidence for a serial killer and in the end being rewarded the game demo. In 2016 Quantic Dream announced that a remaster of Heavy Rain would be released for the PS4 on 1st of March, that same month Quantic Dream released Heavy Rain and their other game Beyond: Two Souls as a collection.
